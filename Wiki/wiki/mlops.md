# MLOps

**Summary**: MLOps is the set of practices used to build, deploy, monitor, and maintain machine learning systems in production. In this wiki, it is described as the combination of machine learning, DevOps, and data engineering. 

**Sources**: `raw/The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md`

**Last updated**: 2026-04-19

---

The source defines MLOps as the discipline that unifies machine learning, DevOps, and data engineering to streamline the end-to-end lifecycle of ML models, from data preparation and experimentation to deployment, monitoring, and optimization (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md). It positions MLOps as the bridge between building models and operationalizing them in production (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md).

## Goals

According to the source, MLOps aims to make ML systems scalable, reproducible, automated, and reliable in production (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md). These goals depend on strong data handling, repeatable environments, deployment workflows, and continuous monitoring (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md).

## Lifecycle Coverage

The article treats MLOps as covering the full lifecycle: data collection, preprocessing, model development, deployment, monitoring, and retraining (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md). This framing overlaps with concepts discussed in [[ml-ci-cd]], [[experiment-tracking]], [[model-drift]], and [[continuous-training]].

## Adjacent Disciplines

The source says MLOps should be understood in relation to DevOps and DataOps, though it does not provide a formal comparison table (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md). It also distinguishes MLOps from ModelOps by saying MLOps covers the full machine learning lifecycle while ModelOps focuses mainly on deployment, governance, and production management of models (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md).

The DevOps roadmap source defines [[devops]] as a software delivery and operations discipline centered on collaboration, CI/CD, infrastructure automation, cloud platforms, containers, and orchestration (source: Complete DevOps Roadmap - Beginner to Advanced.md). In the wiki, this helps clarify that MLOps builds on DevOps ideas but extends them into model development, data workflows, experimentation, and production ML lifecycle concerns (inference: this is a wiki modeling decision based on both sources).
