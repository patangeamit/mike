# Experiment Tracking

**Summary**: Experiment tracking records metrics, parameters, artifacts, and model versions so ML work can be compared and reproduced. The source treats it as a core MLOps practice for systematic model development. 

**Sources**: `raw/The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md`

**Last updated**: 2026-04-19

---

The source identifies experiment tracking as a central part of model development and experimentation in MLOps (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md). It recommends tools such as MLflow and Weights & Biases for logging metrics, parameters, artifacts, and model versions (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md).

## Why It Matters

According to the article, experiment tracking creates an audit trail that makes experiments easier to compare, repeat, and transition into production (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md). It supports transparency, reproducibility, and faster iteration during model development (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md).
