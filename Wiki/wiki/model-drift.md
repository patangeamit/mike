# Model Drift

**Summary**: Model drift is the decline in model accuracy caused by changes in incoming data or production conditions. The source presents drift detection as a key monitoring responsibility in MLOps. 

**Sources**: `raw/The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md`

**Last updated**: 2026-04-19

---

The source defines model drift as a situation where model accuracy declines because incoming data changes over time (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md). It says MLOps addresses this through continuous monitoring, automated alerts, and retraining pipelines (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md).

## Monitoring Context

In the roadmap, drift detection is grouped with model performance monitoring, logging, and observability during the deployment and monitoring phase (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md). The article also treats data validation and quality checks earlier in the lifecycle as part of preventing unreliable production behavior (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md).
