# MLOps Roadmap 2026

**Summary**: A high-level learning roadmap for becoming an MLOps engineer, covering foundations, data systems, experimentation, deployment, monitoring, cloud platforms, and career preparation. The article emphasizes automation, reproducibility, and production reliability as the core goals of MLOps. 

**Sources**: `raw/The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md`

**Last updated**: 2026-04-19

---

The source defines MLOps as the discipline that combines machine learning, DevOps, and data engineering to automate and manage the ML lifecycle from data preparation through deployment, monitoring, and optimization (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md). It presents MLOps as a way to make ML systems scalable, reproducible, automated, and reliable in production (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md).

## Roadmap Structure

The roadmap is organized into eight phases: foundations, data engineering and versioning, model development and experimentation, ML CI/CD, Kubernetes, deployment and monitoring, cloud ML, and capstone projects with interview preparation (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md). This structure reflects a progression from core concepts to production infrastructure and career readiness (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md).

## Core Skills

The article recommends learning machine learning basics, DevOps principles, cloud fundamentals, and the ML lifecycle early in the journey (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md). It then expands into data pipelines, ETL and ELT, data validation, DVC, feature stores, and metadata management as the data foundation for production ML systems (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md).

For model development, the source highlights Python-based development, experiment tracking, hyperparameter optimization, reproducibility, and testing of ML systems (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md). For delivery and operations, it highlights CI/CD, workflow orchestration, Docker, Kubernetes, model serving, A/B testing, observability, model drift detection, and cloud ML services (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md).

The roadmap explicitly mentions DVC, MLflow, Weights & Biases, GitHub Actions, Jenkins, Airflow, Prefect, Docker, Kubernetes, Kubeflow, FastAPI, Flask, Prometheus, Grafana, AWS SageMaker, Azure ML Studio and AKS, and Google Cloud Vertex AI (source: The Ultimate MLOps Roadmap for 2026 A Complete Guide to Becoming an MLOps Engineer.md).
